# Cumulant Reel — Retention & Distribution Guidelines

Derived from the 2026 research (`RESEARCH.md`) + the audit (`AUDIT.md`). Premium editorial identity; MrBeast-level *principles* (hook, clarity, progression, payoff), not his style.

## The three levers we actually control (confirmed signals)
1. **Watch time** (completion % *and* absolute seconds) — keep every second earning its place; cut ruthlessly.
2. **Sends** (DM reshares) — the #1 growth signal; build a payoff worth forwarding ("the sticker isn't the price").
3. **Saves** — a concrete, useful takeaway people keep ("ask for the buydown").
Secondary: meaningful comments (a real open question), profile visits, article clicks.

## Retention-first story structure (adapt per story; never one template)
`Stop → Curiosity → Orientation → Escalation → Explanation → Consequence → Payoff → Forward close.`
- **Stop (0–2s):** first frame legible *without* animation; state the subject in <1s; one truthful promise.
- Keep the curiosity gap open across each cut ("but…", "the catch…", "here's why").
- **Pace contrast:** fast setup → a beat of near-stillness before the key number → slower payoff.
- **Payoff** resolves the hook with a *useful* understanding; don't repeat the hook.
- **Loop:** tie the final frame to the opening when it doesn't harm the conclusion (we flip the opening sticker to the real price).

## Opening rules
- Open on: a verified number, a sharp comparison, a visual contradiction, or a stakes question. Never the logo, a slow establish, a long title, "did you know", or a generic chart.
- Hook must promise something the reel delivers.

## Length
- Shortest that delivers context→evidence→consequence→payoff. Default sweet spot **20–45s**; let the story decide. (Completion rewards short; absolute watch time still counts, so depth is allowed if retention holds.)

## Motion (fix the audit's #1 leak)
- Add **within-beat motion**: counters, line-draws, dial sweeps, reveals, flips — information should *move*, not just appear.
- A **motion-density curve**, not constant max: high-motion attracts, controlled stillness lands claims.
- Every visual change must carry new info / emphasis / orientation / emotion / transition — never decoration.

## Anti-repetition (enforced by `VISUAL_MEMORY.md`)
- Don't reuse the same main object, transition, camera path, chart style, or signature SFX in consecutive reels.
- New central visual concept per story; keep only the brand constants (black/magenta/white, Neue Haas, the mark).

## Captions
- Short phrase groups, one magenta emphasis word, big mobile type, inside the central safe zone, synced, enough read time. No 4–5-line walls, no all-caps screens, no covering charts.

## Platform hygiene (from research)
- **Original only** (our animation always is). **No watermarks.** **No engagement bait** ("comment X", "tag 3").
- Export **1080×1920, 30fps, H.264/AAC, ~10 Mbps**, faststart, a touch of grain (kills magenta-glow banding). Enable IG "Upload at highest quality." **Separate IG vs FB captions** (don't blind cross-post).
- Cover: subject + headline **central** (IG center-crops the grid).

## Engagement (no bait)
- End on a genuine open question or a useful save/share reason. Never "comment YES", fake controversy, or manufactured binaries.

## Brand
- Restrained close, no long logo animation. "Beyond the Norm. Beneath the Headlines."
