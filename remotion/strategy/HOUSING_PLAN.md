# New Reel — "The New-Home Price That Won't Move"

## Story selection (Phase 4)
Picked over Florida insurance, Iran $300B, and the May trade deficit because it wins on the brief's own criteria:
- **Personal relevance:** housing affordability — nearly everyone has a stake.
- **Hidden-truth surprise:** the "flat" price is an illusion; the real price is down ~13%.
- **Clear winner/loser conflict + hard sourced data** (Census, Lennar, Mortgage News Daily, NAHB).
- **Strong, original visual metaphor:** a frozen sticker vs a growing hidden discount → a price tag that flips.
- **Save/share value:** a contrarian, useful explainer ("the sticker isn't the price; ask for the buydown").

## Final script (warm female, ~44s, 8 beats)
1. The sticker price on new homes says flat. But it just dropped 13%.
2. But they're stuck. Sales are sliding, and unsold homes are piling up.
3. So instead of cutting the sticker, they quietly cut your mortgage rate.
4. On the average home, that's a hidden $48,000 discount.
5. The official price never records it, so the market looks calm while it's quietly cracking.
6. In 2008, builders slashed the sticker. Now they keep it pretty and discount in the dark.
7. But here's the catch. That hidden discount is already shrinking.
8. So the real deals are fading. If you're buying, the sticker isn't the price. Ask for the buydown.
