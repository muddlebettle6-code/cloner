# Existing Reel Audit (4 finished reels)

Honest, demanding review of the four shipped reels. Scores 1–10. The point is to find what's costing attention/retention, not to flatter the work.

## Cross-reel verdict (the pattern that matters most)
The reels got **more polished** over time but **structurally repetitive** and **too long**. The biggest retention leaks are shared:
1. **Length: 67–79s.** Far past the high-completion window. Completion rate is the strongest distribution lever and we're sabotaging it.
2. **Sameness across reels.** Dark+grid background, one-object-per-beat layout, the per-transition-type SFX scheme, big-magenta-number treatment, coin stacks (AI bubble *and* DST), zoom/wipe transitions, and the visual-only logo close all repeat. Consecutive reels feel like the same template re-skinned.
3. **Low *within-beat* motion.** Each beat = one object rotating + a caption revealing + a scene transition. Almost no motion that *develops information inside a beat* (a value counting, a line drawing, a reveal). The eye predicts each beat after ~1s.
4. **Even pacing.** No deliberate fast-setup / slow-payoff contrast. Everything moves at one speed, so nothing lands harder than anything else.
5. **First frame is often mid-transition** (blurred in-animation), wasting the 0.0–0.5s stop window.

## Required fixes (carried into the new system)
- **Cut length** to the shortest that delivers context→evidence→consequence→payoff (target 30–45s for this story; let the story decide).
- **Front-load the stop**: a clean, legible first frame (no in-animation blur) that states the subject in <1s.
- **Add within-beat motion**: counters, line-draws, peels, reveals — information should *move*, not just appear.
- **Pace contrast**: fast setup, a beat of near-stillness before the key number, slower payoff.
- **Kill repetition**: new central object system per story; a **visual-memory ledger** (see VISUAL_MEMORY.md) to block reuse of the same object/transition/SFX in consecutive reels.
- **Compression-proofing**: thicker lines, larger labels, controlled glow, a touch of grain, export 1080 not 4K (IG downscales 4K harshly).
- **Stronger close**: resolve the hook with a useful takeaway + a genuine save/share reason; restrained brand, no question-as-crutch.
