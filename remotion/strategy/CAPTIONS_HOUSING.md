The "resilient" new-home market is hiding something.

In May 2026, the median new-home price was basically flat, the same place it's been for three years. That looks like builder strength. It isn't. Sales are falling and unsold inventory has piled up to about 10 months of supply.

Here's the trick: instead of cutting the sticker price, which shows up in the official data, builders are buying down buyers' mortgage rates, a discount the Census price series can't see. On one large builder's average home, that hidden cut is worth roughly $48,000, or about 13% off what buyers actually pay.

The twist: that hidden discount is already shrinking, from 14.5% to 12.9% of the sale price over three quarters. The best deals may already be behind us.

If you're shopping for new construction, the number on the sign isn't the real price. Ask about the rate buydown.
